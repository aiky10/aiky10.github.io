Comissioned work for UP Town Center. I was tasked as the sole industrial designer along with 2 graphic designers to design directories and interactive installations for different areas of the mall. The brief was to create directories that would be fun to interact with and provide entertainment other than just directions to specific areas of the mall. I was also tasked to help design the branding for these installations as well as a map mural with the 2 graphic designers. Unfortunately, the project has been put on hold due to the COVID-19 Pandemic

The ideas I presented are as follows: The map labyrinth, the drop-off map, the modular pocket directory, the wheel of shops, the sun dial and the sliding puzzle. The concept for the labyrinth was for children and adults alike to have a game to play while looking for areas in the map they wanted to go to. Within the labyrinth, there are steel balls that you can shift around and aim to land on the concaved spots for a challenge. The labyrinth allows for tilting using the knobs on the sides. The dropoff map is a simple map placed on the entrance of the malls. The pocket directories were designed to be easily placed anywhere in the mall. The main feature for them is that they are modular the joints used can be easily taken apart by the admin and boards can easily be swapped. This could also be an opportunity to sell ad space to the different shops in the mall. The wheel of shop has a secondary marketing purpose where shops can be included in the rotation of places to go if the wheel lands on them. It's a fun way for students to decide on place to go especially when a mall like UPTC has so much options to choose from. The wheel could come in multiple pieces to serve different types of shops & restaurants to visit. The human sun dial was designed to have your shadow point to the area you should check out. The platforms will have information of different parts of the malls to vist and doubles as a seating area or a play area for kids. Finally, the sliding puzzle also provides different directional informational while providing a fun & casual experienc of solving a puzzle. The boards can also serve as ad space as well as the panel beneath the sliding boards.
